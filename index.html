3 Elijah was afraid and ran for his life. When he came to Beersheba in Judah, he left his servant there, 4 while he himself went a day’s journey into the wilderness. He came to a broom bush, sat down under it and prayed that he might die.

1 Kings 19:3–4

“I have had enough, Lord,” he said. “Take my life; I am no better than my ancestors.” 5 Then he lay down under the bush and fell asleep.

1 Kings 19:4–5

Reading that passage of Elijah fleeing and praying “take my life” is a bit funny

Not because of the situation but because of the hindsight that I have

You see, Elijah had forgotten all his days that were sunny

While he was stuck in the stormclouds of his “bad”

It’s easy to get stuck in these storms and to not see a way out

But what a relief we have, that we have a God that reaches through the clouds

Whenever I didn’t get into computer science, I was stuck in a storm and lost

I was thinking about transferring schools but now in hindsight, I look back and I laugh

How thankful I am that my God was so intentional and had a plan on my behalf!

Whenever you were considering pharmacy, pushin drugs and treatin the flu

Even before that, in the storm cloud of purpose, look at how far he has brought you!

Hindsight makes the stormy clouds humorous, think about how you chose OT too!

In every step of the way there was someone who has been so intentional in his plan even when we have no clue

The Lord sees our stormy clouds and offers us a foothold in himself

But if we ever fall, like Peter he’ll help us back onto the boat

Like Elijah, he’ll give us big backs food to cope

Or like Mel and Caleb, he’ll remind us of how far he’s carried ourselves

You are so smart and so capable, even if you don’t get any A’s

You are so smart and so capable, which is why you’ll be such a great OT someday

And one day you’ll be able to look back and laugh

Cause with hindsight, it’s easy to realize that eventually all stormy clouds will pass

It’s hard to see but what a gift our stormy clouds have been!

The stormy clouds let us walk on water, they push us in our faith

Those clouds remind us that somebody’s reaching out, offering you a drink

It’s hard to see but what a gift! That our stormy clouds lead us back to him!
